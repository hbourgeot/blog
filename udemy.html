Where I can find udemy coupons?

I do not support illegal, as we know, coupons are legal, so with these you can legally access courses that are paid Udemy, be careful. Also, although this is more oriented to programming, in some sites you will be able to find courses in other areas.

Where I can find Udemy Coupons?

All over the internet you can find many sites that collect the coupons that have come out and show them on their website or Telegram channel, however as we all know, not everything is as it appears to be and some sites can hide trojans or have backdoors on their websites to infect your PC, for that reason, I will tell you about some sites and safe Telegram channels, where you will not have to be suspicious.

So, let's go step by step through the sites I will show you, keep in mind that some sites are in Spanish, you can translate them, as they also offer coupons for courses in English, 75% of the coupons on some sites are in English.

CursosDev: more than just courses for devs.

On this website you can find many coupons, almost anything you want, but you must make sure that it has not expired (as it is expected from a coupon), here you can find courses from building a PC to cybersecurity and ethical hacking. Now, how do I access the coupons to "buy" courses? You can access the coupons section by selecting the 4th option, then, click on the course you want to obtain.

Once on the page of the course you want, you can see next to the name and cover of the course how much time is left on the coupon, and below, there are three buttons, one on the left to see more courses, another on the right to report the expiration of the coupon, and finally the one that matters to us is centered, which says "Get coupon", you click on it for redirect to the Udemy page, where you can continue following the clear steps that Udemy tells you to get the course.

Go to CursosDev

Facialix: Udemy coupons and more!

On facialix, you can find much more than coupons for Udemy, you can also find free courses from other learning platforms, such as edX and Coursera, however, this blog does not only offer courses, it also provides several free books, which are interesting to say the truth, although it also provides tutorials that are related to programming.

Something that has surprised me, is that they have a service called EducaLix, you can select which courses to see and things like that, I would love to tell you more about it, but I don't have enough information (besides that I don't have much confidence in them).

About the courses, the process to use the coupons is very similar to the website we saw before, only that in the lower area of the course page you will find only two buttons (before continuing, you should know that they also offer free Udemy courses, not only coupons), the one on the left, is the one that will take us to the Udemy page with the coupon, it should say "Go to Course", and the button located on the right is one that leads to the Facialix Telegram channel.

Go to Facialix

Telegram channels: more than one channel for redeem coupons

There are more channels in Telegram than I think to redeem coupons, seriously, there are too many, this is because there are for each language, at least that I have been aware of in Spanish there are four channels, but two of these also show you which coupons are in English, this is usable if you want to be bilingual.

To search for channels, just go to the search field and enter "Udemy Coupons" and the channels or groups that match the search term will be displayed, as you can see in the image below, note that I searched in Spanish, so the result may vary.

Keep in mind that some channels can take you to malicious sites, be careful with what you find. As a personal advice, buy the courses, do not use coupons, I have more than 20 courses that I have redeemed with coupons and I do not feel the pressure to finish them since I took them practically for free, instead when paying, you feel more pressure because you spend your money. And do not get carried away with the free courses you find, not all are good.
